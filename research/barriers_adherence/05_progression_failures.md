# 05 — Why Existing Lifters Don't Progress

**Key Finding:** Common reasons lifters stall include lack of progressive overload, excessive junk volume, poor recovery management, inconsistent programming (program hopping), and interference from concurrent activities. Many lifters train hard but without a structured progression plan.

**Implications:** The "trained but stuck" population needs diagnosis, not more information. A service that audits programming (volume, intensity, progression model, recovery) and identifies specific errors can unlock gains that self-directed training cannot.
