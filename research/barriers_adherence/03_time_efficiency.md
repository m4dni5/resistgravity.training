# 03 — Time as a Barrier and Minimal-Dose Resistance Training

**Key Finding:** Time is the most consistently cited barrier to exercise, but evidence shows much of this is perceived rather than absolute. Low-volume RT (1-2 sets, 2-3x/week) produces clinically meaningful strength and health gains, and explicitly minimizing time commitment improves adherence.

---

**Implications:** The "I don't have time" barrier is addressable. Evidence supports that 2-3 sessions/week of 30-45 minutes is sufficient for meaningful adaptation. A service that explicitly targets the time-poor (minimal effective dose, efficient session design) removes the #1 cited barrier.
