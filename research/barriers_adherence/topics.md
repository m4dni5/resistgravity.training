## Phase 2: Barriers, Adherence, and the Gap Between Intention and Action

**Goal:** Understand why people don't start RT, why they quit, and why those who continue often don't progress. Map the behavioral and practical barriers that your service needs to solve.

---

### Key Questions

**Q1. Barriers to Adoption in 40+ Adults**
- What are the most commonly cited reasons non-lifters don't start? (Time, intimidation, knowledge, identity, injury fear)
- How do these barriers differ by age, sex, and activity level?
- What's the evidence on "readiness to change" and how it applies to RT adoption?
- What role does gym intimidation / equipment confusion play?

**Q2. Adherence and Dropout**
- What are the adherence rates for RT programs? How many people quit in the first 3 months? 6 months? 12 months?
- What predicts dropout? (Program complexity, time commitment, lack of results, boredom, injury)
- What predicts long-term adherence? (Simplicity, social support, autonomy, competence, enjoyment)
- Is there evidence that simpler programs have better adherence than complex ones?

**Q3. Time as a Barrier**
- How much of the "I don't have time" barrier is real vs. perceived?
- What's the evidence on time-efficient RT (low volume, minimal dose) as an adherence strategy?
- Does doing less, more consistently, beat doing more sporadically?
- Are there studies on RT adherence when time commitment is explicitly minimized?

**Q4. The On-Ramp Problem**
- What does the evidence say about how beginners should start RT?
- Is there a recommended progression from beginner to intermediate?
- How long does it take for a beginner to see measurable results? (Strength, body composition, health markers)
- What's the evidence on supervised vs. unsupervised training for beginners?

**Q5. Why Existing Lifters Don't Progress**
- What are the most common programming errors that prevent progress?
- How common is "junk volume" (training without progressive overload)?
- What's the evidence on training variety vs. consistency for long-term progress?
- How does concurrent activity (sport, recreation) interfere with RT progress?
- What diagnostic markers indicate a program is failing? (No strength gains, persistent soreness, fatigue, regression)

**Q6. Identity and Psychology**
- How does exercise identity ("I'm a lifter" vs. "I'm a runner") affect RT adoption and adherence?
- What's the evidence on Self-Determination Theory (autonomy, competence, relatedness) applied to RT?
- How do health-focused vs. aesthetics-focused framing affect adherence?
- What role does intrinsic vs. extrinsic motivation play in long-term RT behavior?
