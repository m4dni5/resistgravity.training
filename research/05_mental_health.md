## 05 — Mental Health (Depression & Anxiety)

**Key finding:** Resistance training significantly reduces depressive symptoms across all severity levels. The effect size is clinically meaningful and comparable to psychotherapy and pharmacotherapy for mild-to-moderate depression.

---

### Papers

**1. Association of Efficacy of Resistance Exercise Training With Depressive Symptoms: Meta-analysis and Meta-regression Analysis of Randomized Clinical Trials**
- Gordon BR, McDowell CP, Hallgren M, et al. (2018)
- *JAMA Psychiatry* 75(6):566-576
- https://doi.org/10.1001/jamapsychiatry.2018.0572
- Summary: Landmark JAMA Psychiatry meta-analysis of 33 RCTs (n=1,877). Resistance training significantly reduced depressive symptoms regardless of health status, training volume, or significant improvements in strength. The effect was not moderated by whether participants were formally diagnosed with depression. Clinical effect sizes were large. (401 citations)

**2. The Effects of Aerobic and Resistance Exercise on Depression and Anxiety: Systematic Review With Meta-Analysis**
- Banyard H, Edward K-L, Garvey L, et al. (2025)
- *International Journal of Mental Health Nursing*
- https://doi.org/10.1111/inm.70054
- Summary: Recent meta-analysis comparing exercise modalities. Both aerobic and resistance training reduced depression and anxiety, with no significant difference between modalities. Supports the independent efficacy of resistance training for mental health.

**3. A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis of Resistance Training on Quality of Life, Depression, Muscle Strength, and Functional Exercise Capacity in Older Adults ≥60 Years**
- Khodadad Kashi S, Mirzazadeh Z, Saatchian V (2022)
- *Biological Research for Nursing* 24(4):511-524
- https://doi.org/10.1177/10998004221120945
- Summary: Comprehensive meta-analysis of 26 RCTs. Resistance training significantly improved quality of life, reduced depression scores, increased muscle strength, and improved functional capacity in adults over 60. Supports RT as a holistic intervention for aging well. (86 citations)

**4. The Effects of Exclusively Resistance Training-Based Supervised Programs in People with Depression: Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis of RCTs**
- Carneiro L, Afonso J, Ramirez-Campillo R, et al. (2020)
- *International Journal of Environmental Research and Public Health* 17(18):6715
- https://doi.org/10.3390/ijerph17186715
- Summary: Meta-analysis focused specifically on people with clinical depression. Supervised resistance training programs significantly reduced depressive symptoms, even when resistance training was the only exercise modality.

**5. Resistance Training and Combined Resistance and Aerobic Training as Treatment of Depression and Anxiety Symptoms in Young People: Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis**
- Marinelli R, Parker A, Levinger I, et al. (2024)
- *Early Intervention in Psychiatry* 18(5):e13528
- https://doi.org/10.1111/eip.13528
- Summary: Meta-analysis in young people specifically. Resistance training and combined training both effectively reduced depression and anxiety symptoms in youth. Extends the evidence base beyond older adults.

**6. Impact of Resistance Training Repetition Ranges on Cognitive Function and Mental Health in Older Women: RCT**
- Cunha P, Ribeiro A, Cavalcante E, et al. (2025)
- *Journal of Affective Disorders* 371:120938
- https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jad.2025.120938
- Summary: Recent RCT comparing repetition ranges for effects on mental health. Both moderate (8-12 reps) and higher (15-20 reps) repetition ranges improved cognitive function and mental health outcomes in older women.
