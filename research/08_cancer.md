## 08 — Cancer Prevention & Survivorship

**Key finding:** Resistance training during and after cancer treatment reduces cancer-related fatigue, improves body composition, preserves lean mass, and may reduce recurrence risk. International guidelines now recommend exercise (including RT) for all cancer survivors.

---

### Papers

**1. Exercise Guidelines for Cancer Survivors: Consensus Statement from International Multidisciplinary Roundtable**
- Campbell KL, Winters-Stone KM, Wiskemann J, et al. (2019)
- *Medicine and Science in Sports and Exercise* 51(11):2375-2390
- https://doi.org/10.1249/MSS.0000000000002116
- Summary: Official international consensus guidelines. Recommends aerobic and resistance training for all cancer survivors during and after treatment. Evidence supports improved physical function, quality of life, reduced fatigue, and potentially reduced recurrence risk. Resistance training is specifically recommended for preserving muscle mass during treatment. (Highly influential)

**2. Does Resistance Training Ameliorate Cancer-Related Fatigue in Cancer Survivors? Systematic Review with Meta-Analysis**
- Gray L, Sindall P, Pearson S (2024)
- *Disability and Rehabilitation* 46(15):3324-3336
- https://doi.org/10.1080/09638288.2023.2226408
- Summary: Meta-analysis focused specifically on resistance training and cancer-related fatigue (CRF). RT significantly reduced CRF across cancer types. Resistance training may be preferred over aerobic exercise for fatigue reduction because it also addresses muscle wasting.

**3. Combined Aerobic and Resistance Training Improves Body Composition, Cardiometabolic Risk, and Cancer-Related Indicators in Breast Cancer Patients with Overweight/Obesity: Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis**
- Al-Mhanna SB, Batrakoulis A, Norhayati MN, et al. (2024)
- *Journal of Sports Science & Medicine* 23:366-386
- https://doi.org/10.52082/jssm.2024.366
- Summary: Meta-analysis of breast cancer survivors with obesity. Combined exercise improved body composition, cardiometabolic markers, and cancer-related indicators. Resistance training was critical for preventing lean mass loss during cancer treatment.

**4. Effects of Exercise on Inflammation in Female Survivors of Nonmetastatic Breast Cancer: Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis**
- Bettariga F, Taaffe DR, Borsati A, et al. (2025)
- *Journal of the National Cancer Institute*
- https://doi.org/10.1093/jnci/djaf062
- Summary: Meta-analysis showing exercise (including resistance training) reduces inflammatory markers (CRP, IL-6) in breast cancer survivors. Chronic inflammation is linked to cancer recurrence, so anti-inflammatory effects of exercise may improve survival outcomes.

**5. Resistance Exercise and Breast Cancer-Related Lymphedema: Systematic Review Update and Meta-Analysis**
- Hasenoehrl T, Palma S, Ramazanova D, et al. (2020)
- *Supportive Care in Cancer* 28(10):4965-4974
- https://doi.org/10.1007/s00520-020-05521-x
- Summary: Meta-analysis addressing the concern that resistance training might worsen lymphedema in breast cancer survivors. Found that progressive RT does NOT increase the risk of lymphedema and may actually reduce symptoms. Important evidence countering the outdated advice to avoid lifting after mastectomy.
