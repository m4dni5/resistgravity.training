## 13 — Body Composition

**Key finding:** Resistance training is the most effective exercise modality for improving body composition — increasing lean mass while reducing fat mass. It may not always cause weight loss on the scale, but it dramatically improves the ratio of muscle to fat, which is what matters for health.

---

### Papers

**1. Resistance Training Effectiveness on Body Composition and Body Weight Outcomes in Individuals with Overweight/Obesity Across the Lifespan: Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis**
- Lopez P, Taaffe DR, Galvão DA, et al. (2022)
- *Obesity Reviews* 23(7):e13428
- https://doi.org/10.1111/obr.13428
- Summary: Comprehensive meta-analysis across the lifespan. Resistance training consistently reduced body fat percentage and preserved or increased lean mass in overweight/obese individuals. Key insight: RT may not produce dramatic changes on the body weight scale, but it fundamentally transforms body composition — the health-relevant outcome.

**2. Aerobic or Resistance Exercise, or Both, in Dieting Obese Older Adults**
- Villareal DT, Aguirre L, Gurney AB, et al. (2017)
- *New England Journal of Medicine* 376(20):1943-1955
- https://doi.org/10.1056/NEJMoa1616338
- Summary: Landmark NEJM trial. In obese older adults on a calorie-restricted diet: diet + resistance training maintained lean mass and bone density, while diet alone or diet + aerobic exercise caused significant muscle and bone loss. Proves that weight loss without resistance training accelerates sarcopenia and osteopenia. Essential evidence.

**3. Effect of Creatine Supplementation During Resistance Training on Lean Tissue Mass and Muscular Strength in Older Adults: Meta-Analysis**
- Chilibeck PD, Kaviani M, Candow DG, et al. (2017)
- *Open Access Journal of Sports Medicine* 8:95-106
- https://doi.org/10.2147/OAJSM.S123529
- Summary: Meta-analysis of creatine + resistance training in older adults. Creatine supplementation during RT produced greater gains in lean tissue mass and upper-body strength compared to RT alone. Supports the combination as a strategy to combat age-related muscle loss.

**4. Effects of Combined Aerobic and Resistance Training on Body Composition and Metabolic Health in Children and Adolescents with Overweight or Obesity: Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis**
- Liu X, Li Q, Lu F, et al. (2024)
- *Frontiers in Public Health* 12:1409660
- https://doi.org/10.3389/fpubh.2024.1409660
- Summary: Meta-analysis in youth showing combined exercise improves body composition (reduced fat mass, preserved/increased lean mass) in overweight/obese children and adolescents. Resistance training is a critical component for preserving lean mass during growth.

**5. Nutrition and Exercise Interventions to Improve Body Composition for Persons with Overweight or Obesity Near Retirement Age: Network Meta-Analysis**
- Eglseer D, Traxler M, Embacher S, et al. (2023)
- *Advances in Nutrition* 14(5):1140-1157
- https://doi.org/10.1016/j.advnut.2023.04.001
- Summary: Network meta-analysis for the retirement-age population. Exercise programs with a resistance training component were more effective than diet alone or aerobic exercise alone for improving body composition. Combined nutrition + resistance training was the most effective strategy.

**6. Resistance Training is Medicine: Effects of Strength Training on Health**
- Westcott WL (2012)
- *Current Sports Medicine Reports* 11(4):209-216
- https://doi.org/10.1249/JSR.0b013e31825dabb8
- Summary: Foundational paper showing 10 weeks of resistance training increases lean mass by 1.4 kg and decreases fat mass by 1.8 kg while body weight remains approximately stable. Documents the "recomposition" effect that makes resistance training uniquely valuable.
