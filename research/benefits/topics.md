## Research Topics — Health Benefits of Resistance Training

### Master Topic List (Revised & Expanded)

**Muscle & Aging**
- Sarcopenia: age-related muscle mass and strength loss
- Muscle mass and strength as predictors of all-cause mortality
- Functional capacity and independence in older adults

**Bone & Skeletal Health**
- Resistance training and osteoporosis / bone mineral density
- Fall prevention and balance in older adults

**Brain & Cognitive Health**
- Resistance training and cognitive function (executive function, memory)
- Leg strength / muscle mass as predictors of cognitive decline

**Mental Health**
- Resistance training and depression
- Resistance training and anxiety
- Resistance training and sleep quality

**Cardiovascular Health**
- Resistance training and blood pressure / hypertension
- Resistance training and cardiovascular disease risk reduction
- Resistance training and metabolic syndrome

**Metabolic Health**
- Resistance training and type 2 diabetes / insulin sensitivity
- Resistance training and body composition / obesity
- Resistance training and resting metabolic rate

**Cancer**
- Resistance training for cancer survivors
- Resistance training and cancer-related fatigue
- Resistance training and cancer prevention

**Immune Function & Inflammation**
- Muscle as an endocrine/secretory organ (myokines)
- Resistance training and chronic inflammation (IL-6, CRP, TNF-α)
- Resistance training and immune function

**Dose & Programming**
- Minimum effective dose of resistance training per week
- Dose-response: volume, frequency, intensity for health outcomes
- Optimal training parameters by age group

**Cellular & Molecular Mechanisms**
- Muscle protein synthesis and anabolic signaling
- Autophagy, mitochondrial health, and cellular maintenance
- Hormonal adaptations to resistance training

**Safety & Efficacy Across Populations**
- Injury rates compared to other physical activities
- Safety in youth, elderly, women, pregnant women
- Safety in clinical populations (cardiac, osteoporosis, diabetes, neurological)
- Sex differences in training response
- Racial/ethnic differences in training response
