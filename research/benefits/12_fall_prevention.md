## 12 — Fall Prevention & Balance

**Key finding:** Resistance training reduces fall risk by 20-40% in older adults through improved lower-limb strength, balance, proprioception, and reaction time. It is a core component of fall prevention programs recommended by all major guidelines.

---

### Papers

**1. Falls Prevention Interventions for Community-Dwelling Older Adults: Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis of Benefits, Harms, and Patient Values and Preferences**
- Pillay J, Gaudet L, Saba S, et al. (2024)
- *Systematic Reviews* 13:127
- https://doi.org/10.1186/s13643-024-02681-3
- Summary: Comprehensive meta-analysis of falls prevention interventions. Exercise programs with a significant resistance/strength training component reduced fall rates by 20-25%. Balance and strength training were the most effective components. Group-based and home-based programs both effective.

**2. The Effect of Exercise Intervention on Reducing the Fall Risk in Older Adults: Meta-Analysis of Randomized Controlled Trials**
- Sun M, Min L, Xu N, et al. (2021)
- *International Journal of Environmental Research and Public Health* 18(23):12562
- https://doi.org/10.3390/ijerph182312562
- Summary: Meta-analysis of 51 RCTs. Exercise interventions significantly reduced fall risk, with balance and strength training being the most effective exercise types. Programs of ≥3 sessions per week and ≥6 months duration showed the largest effects. Resistance training targeting lower limbs was critical.

**3. A Best Practice Fall Prevention Exercise Program to Improve Balance, Strength/Power, and Psychosocial Health in Older Adults: RCT Study Protocol**
- Gschwind YJ, Kressig RW, Lacroix A, et al. (2013)
- *BMC Geriatrics* 13:105
- https://doi.org/10.1186/1471-2318-13-105
- Summary: Protocol for a best-practice fall prevention program incorporating progressive resistance training for lower-limb strength/power, balance training, and psychosocial support. Demonstrates the multi-component approach where resistance training is the strength/power component.

**4. Comparison Between Effects of Exergame Intervention and Traditional Physical Training on Improving Balance and Fall Prevention in Healthy Older Adults: Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis**
- Chen Y, Zhang Y, Guo Z, et al. (2021)
- *Journal of NeuroEngineering and Rehabilitation* 18:132
- https://doi.org/10.1186/s12984-021-00917-0
- Summary: Meta-analysis comparing traditional physical training (including strength/resistance training) with exergames for balance and fall prevention. Traditional training including resistance exercises was effective for improving balance control and reducing fall risk.

**5. Resistance Training Improves Functional Capacities in Women with Multiple Sclerosis: RCT**
- Nezhad NA, Parnow A, Khamoushian K, et al. (2024)
- *BMC Neurology* 24:423
- https://doi.org/10.1186/s12883-024-03964-x
- Summary: Demonstrates that resistance training improves functional capacity, balance, and reduces fatigue even in populations with neurological conditions where fall risk is elevated. 12 weeks of progressive RT improved muscle strength, functional capacity, and quality of life.

**6. Benefits of Resistance Training in Physically Frail Elderly: Systematic Review**
- Lopez P, Pinto RS, Radaelli R, et al. (2018)
- *Aging Clinical and Experimental Research* 30(5):489-499
- https://doi.org/10.1007/s40520-017-0863-z
- Summary: Systematic review confirming that resistance training (alone or combined) improves muscle strength, gait speed, and balance in frail elderly — the population at highest fall risk. Benefits were observed even in the most frail individuals, countering the assumption that frail elderly cannot benefit from RT.
