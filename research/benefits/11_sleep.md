## 11 — Sleep Quality

**Key finding:** Resistance training improves sleep quality, reduces sleep onset latency, and decreases nighttime awakenings. The effect is comparable to or better than aerobic exercise for sleep improvement, particularly in older adults and those with insomnia.

---

### Papers

**1. Comparative Efficacy of Exercise Regimens on Sleep Quality in Older Adults: Systematic Review and Network Meta-Analysis**
- Hasan F, Tu Y-K, Lin C-M, et al. (2022)
- *Sleep Medicine Reviews* 65:101673
- https://doi.org/10.1016/j.smrv.2022.101673
- Summary: Network meta-analysis of 24 RCTs comparing exercise types for sleep quality in older adults. Resistance training, yoga, and tai chi were the most effective modalities. Resistance training specifically improved Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index (PSQI) scores significantly. Combined exercise (resistance + aerobic) was most effective overall.

**2. Effects of Resistance Exercise Training and Stretching on Chronic Insomnia**
- D'Aurea C, Poyares D, Passos G, et al. (2019)
- *Revista Brasileira de Psiquiatria* 41(4):315-322
- https://doi.org/10.1590/1516-4446-2018-0030
- Summary: RCT comparing resistance training, stretching, and waitlist control in chronic insomnia patients. Resistance training significantly improved sleep quality (PSQI), sleep efficiency, and sleep onset latency compared to both stretching and control groups. Mood and quality of life also improved.

**3. Exercise Training Improves Sleep Quality in Middle-Aged and Older Adults with Sleep Problems: Systematic Review**
- Yang P-Y, Ho K-H, Chen H-C, Chien M-Y (2012)
- *Journal of Physiotherapy* 58(5):297-304
- https://doi.org/10.1016/S1836-9553(12)70106-6
- Summary: Systematic review and meta-analysis of 13 RCTs. Exercise training (including resistance training) significantly improved subjective and objective sleep quality in middle-aged and older adults with sleep problems. Effects were equivalent to pharmacotherapy for sleep improvement.

**4. Impact of Resistance Training on Sleep Quality, Mental Health, and Functional Capacity in Older Women with Varying Baseline Sleep Quality: RCT**
- Cunha PM, Santos LD, Herold F, et al. (2025)
- *Psychology of Sport and Exercise* 78:102968
- https://doi.org/10.1016/j.psychsport.2025.102968
- Summary: Recent RCT demonstrating that resistance training improved sleep quality regardless of baseline sleep quality in older women. Those with poorest baseline sleep benefited most. Also improved mental health and functional capacity — supporting resistance training as a holistic intervention for aging well.

**5. Resistance Training and Sleep: A Narrative Review of the Potential Mechanisms**
- Kovacevic A, Mavros Y, Heisz JJ, Singh MAF (2018)
- *Sleep Medicine Reviews* 37:41-52
- Summary: Narrative review of mechanisms by which resistance training improves sleep. Proposed mechanisms include: increased adenosine accumulation, body temperature regulation, anxiety reduction, circadian rhythm regulation, increased slow-wave sleep, and reduced inflammatory markers that disrupt sleep.
