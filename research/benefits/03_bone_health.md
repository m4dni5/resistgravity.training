## 03 — Bone Health & Osteoporosis

**Key finding:** Progressive resistance training increases bone mineral density (BMD) at loaded sites, particularly the lumbar spine and femoral neck. This is critical for preventing osteoporotic fractures, especially in postmenopausal women.

---

### Papers

**1. Progressive Resistance Training for Concomitant Increases in Muscle Strength and Bone Mineral Density in Older Adults: Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis**
- O'Bryan SJ, Giuliano C, Woessner M, et al. (2022)
- *Sports Medicine* 52(8):1813-1831
- https://doi.org/10.1007/s40279-022-01675-2
- Summary: Meta-analysis of 24 RCTs (n=1,661). Progressive resistance training simultaneously improved both muscle strength and bone mineral density in older adults, demonstrating the dual benefit of RT for musculoskeletal health. Moderate-to-high intensity was most effective. (84 citations)

**2. The Effect of Resistance Training on Bone Mineral Density in Older Adults: Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis**
- Massini D, Nedog F, de Oliveira T, et al. (2022)
- *Healthcare* 10(6):1129
- https://doi.org/10.3390/healthcare10061129
- Summary: Meta-analysis confirming that resistance training is an effective stimulus for bone mineral formation. Recommends RT to decrease the rate of age-related bone loss and reduce fracture risk.

**3. Optimal Resistance Training Parameters for Improving Bone Mineral Density in Postmenopausal Women: Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis**
- Zhao F, Su W-W, Sun Y, et al. (2025)
- *Journal of Orthopaedic Surgery and Research* 20:341
- https://doi.org/10.1186/s13018-025-05890-1
- Summary: Meta-analysis specifically for postmenopausal women. Found that resistance training at ≥70% 1RM, 2-3 sessions/week, for ≥6 months significantly improved BMD at the lumbar spine and femoral neck. Provides specific prescription guidelines.

**4. Comparative Efficacy of Different Resistance Training Protocols on BMD in Postmenopausal Women: Network Meta-Analysis**
- Wang Z, Zan X, Li Y, et al. (2023)
- *Frontiers in Physiology* 14:1105303
- https://doi.org/10.3389/fphys.2023.1105303
- Summary: Network meta-analysis comparing RT protocols. Combined high-load and low-load protocols, and multi-joint exercises, were most effective for BMD improvement in postmenopausal women.

**5. The Clinician's Guide to Prevention and Treatment of Osteoporosis**
- LeBoff MS, Greenspan SL, Insogna KL, et al. (2022)
- *Osteoporosis International* 33:1-43
- https://doi.org/10.1007/s00198-021-05900-y
- Summary: Official clinical guidelines from the National Osteoporosis Foundation. Weight-bearing exercise and resistance training are recommended as first-line interventions for osteoporosis prevention and treatment alongside calcium/vitamin D supplementation. Resistance training is specifically highlighted for improving bone density and reducing fall risk.

**6. Sarcopenia, Osteoporosis and Frailty**
- Gielen E, Dupont J, Dejaeger M, et al. (2023)
- *Metabolism: Clinical and Experimental* 141:155638
- https://doi.org/10.1016/j.metabol.2023.155638
- Summary: Review of the interconnection between muscle loss (sarcopenia) and bone loss (osteoporosis) — termed "osteosarcopenia." Resistance training is the only intervention that addresses both conditions simultaneously.
