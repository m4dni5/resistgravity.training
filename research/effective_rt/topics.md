## Phase 1: What Makes Resistance Training Effective for Health

**Goal:** Identify the specific programming variables that produce health outcomes in generally active adults — not athletes, not clinical populations. What works, how much is enough, and what doesn't matter.

---

### Key Questions

**Q1. Minimum Effective Dose for Health**
- What is the lowest volume/frequency of RT that produces meaningful health outcomes (strength, BMD, metabolic markers, mortality risk reduction)?
- Is there a threshold below which RT provides no benefit?
- How does "minimum effective dose" differ by outcome (strength vs. hypertrophy vs. bone vs. metabolic)?

**Q2. Programming Variables That Matter**
- Volume (sets per muscle group per week): what's the dose-response curve?
- Frequency (sessions per muscle group per week): is 1x enough? Is 2x always better?
- Intensity (% of 1RM): does it need to be heavy? Can lighter loads produce health outcomes?
- Exercise selection: compound vs. isolation — which movements matter most for health?
- Progression: does it need to be progressive overload? What happens without it?
- Tempo, rest periods, rep ranges: do these matter for health, or only for performance?

**Q3. Concurrent Training — RT + Aerobic/Sport Activity**
- How does adding RT to an existing aerobic/endurance routine affect outcomes?
- What is the interference effect, when does it happen, and how bad is it?
- How should RT be programmed alongside skiing, cycling, running, hiking?
- What's the evidence on combined vs. sequential training sessions?

**Q4. Movement Patterns and Exercise Selection**
- What movement patterns are most important for health? (Squat, hinge, push, pull, carry, single-leg?)
- Are machines sufficient, or do free weights/compound movements produce better outcomes?
- What does the evidence say about functional training vs. traditional strength training for health?
- Are there specific exercises with the best risk-benefit ratio for 40+ adults?

**Q5. Recovery and Adaptation**
- How does recovery capacity change with age?
- What's the evidence on recovery markers (sleep, HRV, soreness) and training readiness?
- How does total life stress (work, sleep, other activity) affect RT outcomes?
- What's the minimum recovery time between sessions?
