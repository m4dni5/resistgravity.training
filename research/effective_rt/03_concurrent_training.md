# Concurrent Training: RT + Aerobic/Sport Activity

## Concurrent Training

**Key Finding:** Adding RT to an aerobic routine enhances overall health outcomes with minimal interference for health-focused goals. The interference effect is real but modest, primarily affecting maximal strength and hypertrophy when endurance volume is very high. For active adults doing recreational sport, RT can be combined with endurance training without meaningful interference when sessions are spaced appropriately.

### Papers

**1. Concurrent training: a meta-analysis examining interference of aerobic and resistance exercises.**
- Jacob Wilson, Pedro Marin, Matthew Rhea et al. (2012)
- *Journal of strength and conditioning research*
- https://doi.org/10.1519/JSC.0b013e31823a3e2d
- Summary: Meta-analysis of 21 studies examining which endurance training components interfere with RT outcomes. Running (not cycling) was the primary modality causing interference. Concurrent training did not significantly impair hypertrophy but did attenuate maximal strength gains. Longer endurance sessions and higher frequencies increased interference.

**2. Concurrent Strength and Endurance Training: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis on the Impact of Sex and Training Status.**
- Raven Huiberts, Rob Wüst, Stephan van der Zwaard (2024)
- *Sports medicine (Auckland, N.Z.)*
- https://doi.org/10.1007/s40279-023-01943-9
- Summary: Updated meta-analysis examining how sex and training status modulate the interference effect. Found that the interference effect on strength was more pronounced in trained individuals. Females may experience less interference than males. Untrained individuals showed minimal interference from concurrent training.

**3. Comparison of concurrent, resistance, or aerobic training on body fat loss: a systematic review and meta-analysis.**
- Kworweinski Lafontant, Alexa Rukstela, Ardis Hanson et al. (2025)
- *Journal of the International Society of Sports Nutrition*
- https://doi.org/10.1080/15502783.2025.2507949
- Summary: Compared RT, aerobic training, and concurrent training for body fat loss. Concurrent training produced similar fat loss to aerobic training alone, while RT preserved more lean mass during fat loss. Supports the use of concurrent training for body composition goals in active adults.

**4. The Effects of Concurrent Aerobic and Strength Training on Muscle Fiber Hypertrophy: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis.**
- Tommy Lundberg, Joshua Feuerbacher, Marvin Sünkeler et al. (2022)
- *Sports medicine (Auckland, N.Z.)*
- https://doi.org/10.1007/s40279-022-01688-x
- Summary: Examined fiber-level hypertrophy under concurrent training. Found that concurrent training did not impair whole muscle hypertrophy compared to RT alone. At the myofiber level, type I fiber hypertrophy may be slightly attenuated but type II fibers showed similar growth. The interference at the cellular level is less than previously feared.

**5. Does Sprint Interval Training Cause Interference in Concurrent Training? A Meta-Analysis Study.**
- Diego Ferraro-Farro, Marcelo Bandeira-Guimarães, Eduarda Blanco-Rambo et al. (2026)
- *International journal of sports medicine*
- https://doi.org/10.1055/a-2820-4527
- Summary: Examined whether sprint interval training (SIT) as the endurance component causes less interference than moderate-intensity continuous training. Found that SIT-based concurrent training produced less interference on strength gains, likely because SIT shares more physiological overlap with RT. Supports using higher-intensity, shorter-duration endurance work when combining with RT.

**6. Comparison of Power Training vs Traditional Strength Training on Physical Function in Older Adults: A Systematic Review and Meta-analysis.**
- Anoop Balachandran, James Steele, Daniel Angielczyk et al. (2022)
- *JAMA network open*
- https://doi.org/10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2022.11623
- Summary: Compared power training (fast concentric) vs traditional strength training for functional outcomes in older adults. Both modalities improved physical function, but power training showed advantages for functional tasks requiring speed. Relevant for concurrent training programming when sport-specific power is a goal alongside general health.
