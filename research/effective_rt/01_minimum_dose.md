# Minimum Effective Dose for Health

## Minimum Effective Dose

**Key Finding:** As little as 1 set per muscle group per week can produce meaningful strength gains, but the dose-response curve shows benefits increase with additional volume up to ~10 sets/week. For mortality risk reduction, even modest RT participation (1-2 sessions/week) is associated with significantly lower all-cause and cardiovascular mortality.

### Papers

**1. Dose-response relationship between weekly resistance training volume and increases in muscle mass: A systematic review and meta-analysis.**
- Brad Schoenfeld, Dan Ogborn, James Krieger (2017)
- *Journal of sports sciences*
- https://doi.org/10.1080/02640414.2016.1210197
- Summary: Meta-regression of 15 studies (34 treatment groups) examining weekly set volume and muscle mass gains. Found a clear dose-response relationship where each additional set per muscle group per week was associated with additional hypertrophy. Suggests diminishing returns beyond ~10 sets/week per muscle group.

**2. Resistance Training and Mortality Risk: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis.**
- Prathiyankara Shailendra, Katherine Baldock, L Li et al. (2022)
- *American journal of preventive medicine*
- https://doi.org/10.1016/j.amepre.2022.03.020
- Summary: Systematic review and meta-analysis of RT and all-cause, cardiovascular, and cancer mortality. Found that RT participation was associated with significant reductions in all-cause mortality risk. Even 1-2 sessions per week showed meaningful risk reduction, with U-shaped dose-response curves suggesting diminishing returns at very high volumes.

**3. Single vs. multiple sets of resistance exercise for muscle hypertrophy: a meta-analysis.**
- James Krieger (2010)
- *Journal of strength and conditioning research*
- https://doi.org/10.1519/JSC.0b013e3181d4d436
- Summary: Multi-level meta-regression comparing single vs. multiple sets per exercise on muscle hypertrophy. Multiple sets were associated with a larger effect size than single sets (ES difference of 0.32). However, single sets still produced meaningful hypertrophy, confirming a minimum effective dose exists.

**4. The Minimum Effective Training Dose Required to Increase 1RM Strength in Resistance-Trained Men: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis.**
- Patroklos Androulakis-Korakakis, James Fisher, James Steele (2020)
- *Sports medicine (Auckland, N.Z.)*
- https://doi.org/10.1007/s40279-019-01236-0
- Summary: Examined the minimum volume needed to increase 1RM strength in already-trained individuals. Found that even very low volumes (as few as 1-2 sets per week at sufficient intensity) can maintain or modestly increase strength, though more volume is needed for continued gains. The minimum effective dose is lower for strength than for hypertrophy.

**5. Weight training and risk of all-cause, cardiovascular disease and cancer mortality among older adults.**
- Prathiyankara Shailendra, Katherine Baldock, Lok Li et al. (2024)
- *International journal of epidemiology*
- https://doi.org/10.1093/ije/dyae074
- Summary: Large prospective study examining dose-response associations between weight training and mortality in older adults. Found significant risk reductions for all-cause and CVD mortality with as little as 30-60 minutes of weight training per week. Benefits plateaued or slightly declined at very high volumes.

**6. Resistance Training Volume Enhances Muscle Hypertrophy but Not Strength in Trained Men.**
- Brad Schoenfeld, Bret Contreras, James Krieger et al. (2019)
- *Medicine and science in sports and exercise*
- https://doi.org/10.1249/MSS.0000000000001764
- Summary: Compared low (1 set), moderate (3 sets), and high (5 sets) volume RT in trained men over 8 weeks. High-volume training produced significantly greater hypertrophy than low-volume, but strength gains were similar across all groups. Suggests minimum effective dose for strength is lower than for hypertrophy.
