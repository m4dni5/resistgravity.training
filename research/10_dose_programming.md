## 10 — Dose & Programming (Minimum Effective Dose)

**Key finding:** As little as 1-2 sessions per week of resistance training produces significant health benefits. For strength and hypertrophy, 2-3 sessions per week with 2-6 sets per muscle group is optimal. Higher frequency and volume provide additional, diminishing returns.

---

### Papers

**1. The Resistance Training Dose Response: Meta-Regressions Exploring the Effects of Weekly Volume and Frequency on Muscle Hypertrophy and Strength Gains**
- Pelland J, Remmert J, Robinson Z, et al. (2026)
- *Sports Medicine* (ahead of print)
- https://doi.org/10.1007/s40279-025-02344-w
- Summary: Latest meta-regression analyzing the dose-response relationship. Higher weekly set volume enhances hypertrophy and strength, with a threshold of approximately 10+ sets per muscle group per week for maximal hypertrophy. Frequency of 2-3x per week per muscle group is optimal. Evidence supports both high and low frequency approaches if total volume is matched.

**2. How Many Times Per Week Should a Muscle Be Trained to Maximize Muscle Hypertrophy? Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis**
- Schoenfeld BJ, Grgic J, Krieger J (2019)
- *Journal of Sports Sciences* 37(11):1286-1295
- https://doi.org/10.1080/02640414.2018.1555906
- Summary: Meta-analysis of 10 studies. Training a muscle 2x per week produced significantly superior hypertrophy compared to 1x per week. Dose-response data suggest the minimum effective frequency is 2 sessions per muscle group per week for maximal muscle growth.

**3. Dose-Response Modelling of Resistance Exercise Across Outcome Domains: Meta-Analysis**
- Swinton P, Schoenfeld BJ, Murphy A (2024)
- *Sports Medicine* 54(4):913-928
- https://doi.org/10.1007/s40279-024-02006-3
- Summary: Comprehensive dose-response modeling across strength, hypertrophy, power, and endurance outcomes. Demonstrates that even low volumes of resistance training produce meaningful health benefits. The relationship between volume and adaptation is non-linear, with the largest gains occurring in the first few sets.

**4. Resistance Training Volume on Physical Function, Lean Body Mass and Lower-Body Muscle Hypertrophy and Strength in Older Adults: Network Meta-Analysis of 151 RCTs**
- Radaelli R, Rech A, Molinari T, et al. (2025)
- *Sports Medicine* 55:151-168
- https://doi.org/10.1007/s40279-024-02123-z
- Summary: For older adults specifically: 2-6 sets per muscle group per week, performed 2-3 times weekly, optimizes lean body mass and functional capacity. Even low volumes (1-2 sets) produce significant improvements over no training. Provides practical guidance for time-limited older adults.

**5. Resistance Training is Medicine: Effects of Strength Training on Health**
- Westcott WL (2012)
- *Current Sports Medicine Reports* 11(4):209-216
- https://doi.org/10.1249/JSR.0b013e31825dabb8
- Summary: Establishes that just 10 weeks of resistance training (2-3 sessions per week) produces measurable improvements in lean mass (+1.4 kg), resting metabolic rate (+7%), fat mass (-1.8 kg), blood pressure, bone density, and glucose metabolism. Provides the minimum effective "prescription."

**6. Global Consensus on Optimal Exercise Recommendations for Enhancing Healthy Longevity in Older Adults (ICFSR)**
- Izquierdo M, de Souto Barreto P, Arai H, et al. (2025)
- *The Journal of Nutrition, Health & Aging*
- https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jnha.2024.100401
- Summary: International consensus recommends: progressive resistance training 2-3x/week, balance training 2-3x/week, and moderate aerobic activity 150+ min/week. Even 1 session of resistance training per week is beneficial and preferable to none. The best program is one that people will actually do consistently.
