# Weekly Self-Survey — Everfit Check-In

Ready to build in Everfit. 5 scored questions (Linear Scale 0-5) + 1 short answer.

---

## Questions

### Q1. SLEEP

**Type:** Linear Scale 0-5
**Question:** "How would you rate your sleep quality this week?"

- 0 = Very poor
- 5 = Excellent

**Why this question:** Sleep quality is the most important recovery variable for 40+ adults. Sleep deprivation impairs force production, reduces testosterone and GH secretion, and increases cortisol — all of which compromise strength adaptations. (Knowles et al., 2018)

---

### Q2. DAY-AFTER SORENESS / FATIGUE

**Type:** Linear Scale 0-5
**Question:** "How did you feel after each of your workouts?"

- 0 = Extremely sore or fatigued, interfered with daily life
- 1 = Very sore, took 2+ days to recover
- 2 = Noticeably sore
- 3 = Moderate,  felt it but manageable
- 4 = Mild, faded within a day
- 5 = Felt fine, bounced back quickly

**Why this question:** Older adults experience prolonged soreness and force decrements from exercise-induced muscle damage. Recovery time course is a key signal for whether the training dose is appropriate for their current capacity. (Clifford, 2019; Morán-Navarro et al., 2017)

---

### Q3. STRESS

**Type:** Linear Scale 0-5
**Question:** "How manageable was your overall life stress this week?"

- 0 = Overwhelming
- 5 = All good

**Why this question:** Sleep quality, nutrition, and total life stress are stronger determinants of recovery than age alone. High stress compromises adaptation independently of training quality. (05_recovery_adaptation.md)

---

### Q4. ENJOYMENT

**Type:** Linear Scale 0-5
**Question:** "How much did you enjoy training this week?"

- 0 = It was a chore
- 5 = I looked forward to each session

**Why this question:** Boredom and dread are top dropout predictors. Intrinsic motivation (enjoyment, competence) outperforms extrinsic goals (aesthetics) for long-term maintenance. (02_adherence_dropout.md; 06_identity_psychology.md)

---

### Q5. SUBJECTIVE PROGRESS

**Type:** Linear Scale 0-5
**Question:** "Do you feel like you're making progress toward your goals?"

- 0 = Not at all
- 3 = Somewhat
- 5 = Crushing it

**Why this question:** Perceived lack of progress is the top dropout predictor, independent of actual progress. Someone can be completing all prescribed work and still feel stuck. This also serves as a competence signal per Self-Determination Theory. (02_adherence_dropout.md; 06_identity_psychology.md)

---

### Q6. ACTIVITY & NOTES

**Type:** Short Answer (optional)
**Question:** "What other physical activities did you do this week (running, hiking, skiing, etc.)? Anything else you want me to know?"

**Why this question:** Concurrent training interference — running and high-volume endurance work attenuate strength gains. Framed as open so client can list specifics, which gives Matt context for interpreting low progress or recovery scores. (Wilson et al., 2012; Huiberts et al., 2024)

---

## Scoring

Total score: Q1-Q5, max 25.

### Flag for review if:

- Total score <= 15 (60%) in any single week
- Any single question scores 0 or 1
- Q4 (Enjoyment) or Q5 (Progress) drops 2+ points from prior week

### How to read the scores:

**Recovery block (Q1-Q3):** If sleep, soreness, and stress are all low, the client is under-recovering. Options: reduce volume, add rest days, address sleep hygiene, or reduce concurrent activity.

**Psychology block (Q4-Q5):** If enjoyment drops, the program may need variety or autonomy. If subjective progress drops but objective Everfit data shows gains, the client needs reframing or a visible win.

**Context (Q6):** If recovery scores are low and Q6 shows heavy skiing or trail running, the interference effect may be the cause — not the program itself.
