# On-Ramp Program Design

## AGENTS.md — Context for Subagents

### What This Is

Designing the on-ramp program for Audience A: adults 40+ with no current RT routine who need to start.

### Research Inputs

From Phase 2 (barriers_adherence/):
- `01_barriers_adoption.md` — Time, intimidation, knowledge, injury fear are the main barriers
- `02_adherence_dropout.md` — Simplicity predicts adherence; complexity predicts dropout
- `03_time_efficiency.md` — 1-2 sets/week per muscle group produces meaningful gains
- `04_onramp_beginners.md` — First 8 weeks are critical; supervision improves outcomes
- `06_identity_psychology.md` — Identity ("I'm a lifter") predicts maintenance better than goals

From Phase 1 (effective_rt/):
- `01_minimum_dose.md` — 2 sessions/week, 2-3 sets per movement pattern is sufficient
- `04_exercise_selection.md` — Compound movements, free weights or machines, keep it simple

From Benefits (benefits/):
- `00_summary.md` — The persuasive case for RT (for the guide/copy)

### Key Design Constraints

- Target: 40+ adults, no current RT routine, mostly active outdoor people (Truckee/Tahoe)
- Time budget: 2-3 sessions/week, 30-45 minutes
- Equipment: Trout Creek Recreation Center (in-person), or home/minimal equipment (hybrid/remote)
- Matt's background: NSCA-CPT, CF-L2, USAW, Kettlebell Sport; mountain guide, SAR
- Business model: In-person per session + packages; online monthly fee via Everfit

### Deliverable
